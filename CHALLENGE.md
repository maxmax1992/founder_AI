**Format:** working prototype

## The feeling this challenge is about

1pm. You have a hard conversation with your co-founder tomorrow and you don't know how to frame it. You have a question you're embarrassed to ask your mentor. You're not sure if what you're feeling is a real signal or just noise.

There's no one to call.

That's the gap. And it exists in every cohort, every year, no matter how good the program is.

## What you're building

**Sprint Buddy** — an AI companion that travels with every Aalto Founder Sprint participant through their entire 15-week journey.

Not a chatbot. Not a survey tool. A coach in their pocket — available at 11pm, before the hard conversation, in the silence after a bad pitch.

Sprint Buddy starts with one advisor: **Mårten Mickos**, Head of Aalto Founder School and former CEO of MySQL and HackerOne. His actual frameworks, mental models, and hard-won lessons — distilled into a conversational AI that gives guidance grounded in real operator experience, not generic startup advice.

The architecture you build should be designed to grow. Mårten is the first coach. The platform should be able to onboard the next one.

## What to build

### 1. The Mårten advisor ← required

A conversational AI loaded with Mårten's actual knowledge base — provided to you directly. When a founder is stuck, they ask. The buddy responds with guidance grounded in real operator experience. Design it so adding the next advisor is a matter of uploading new material, not rebuilding the system.

### 2. Daily check-ins ← required

2–3 questions a day – the uncomfortable ones founders usually avoid. Not a form, not a survey. The buddy's job is to help founders notice what they'd otherwise suppress. Over time, it reflects patterns back: *"You've mentioned vision alignment three times this week – want to dig into that?"* Text or voice. Make it feel like a coach.

### 3. Founder profiling ← if you nail the core

Help each participant understand their strengths, blind spots, and the role they're best suited for on a team. A strong starting point: the 6 Types of Working Genius. This is what makes Sprint Buddy feel like a personal growth tool, not a productivity tracker.

### 4. Organizer signal ← if you nail the core

Not a dashboard – one simple thing: a weekly view of who might need attention. Human-in-the-loop. The buddy surfaces patterns, the Founder Sprint team makes judgment calls. Participants must know clearly what stays private and what doesn't.

If you come up with something that would benefit the sprint, surprise us.

**The hardest design problem** – If participants feel monitored, they'll perform instead of reflect. The best solutions make founders want to be honest – because Sprint Buddy helps them, not just the organizers. Solve this and you've solved the hard part.

## How we'll judge it

The judges are the Aalto Founder Sprint team. We're evaluating whether we'd trust this with our next cohort.

Each judge scores every criterion 1–5. Scores are weighted and averaged across all judges. Maximum total: 5.0

| Criterion | Weight | What a 5 looks like |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Founder experience | 30% | A founder going through Sprint would open this daily without being asked |
| AI integration | 25% | The advisor gives guidance that feels genuinely useful, not a glorified FAQ |
| Technical execution | 20% | Runs stably, demoed without breaking, clearly deployable |
| Extensibility | 15% | Adding a second advisor is a matter of uploading materials, not rebuilding |
| Wow factor | 10% | Makes the team say "we need this but didn't think of it" |

**Score anchors – calibrate before judging:**

- 1 – Missing or broken
- 2 – Attempted but doesn't land
- 3 – Solid, does the job
- 4 – Strong, clearly thought through
- 5 – Exceptional – would deploy this tomorrow

Anything above 3.5 is genuinely strong work for 48 hours.
